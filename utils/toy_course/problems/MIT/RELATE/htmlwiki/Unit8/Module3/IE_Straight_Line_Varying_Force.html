Illustrative Example: Straight Line Motion with a Force of Changing Magnitude

A block is moving along a smooth horizontal surface when it encounters a rough region. In this region the coefficient of kinetic friction between the block and the surface depends on position and is given by μ(x)=bx, where b is a positive constant (units m-1). What is the work done by the force of friction on the block after it has moved a distance d along the rough surface?

Answer: [show]

The work is \( -b mg\frac{d^2}{2}\)

Solution:

Consider the coordinate system as shown in the figure. The origin of the x-axis is where the rough region starts, therefore the work done by the force of friction on the box after it has moved a distance d is given by:

\[ W_{d,0} = \int_{0}^{d} \vec{f} \cdot \; d \vec{r} \]

The forces on the block along the vertical direction are the normal, N, and gravity, mg. Because there is no motion along the vertical axis, ΣFy = 0, implies N = mg . Then the magnitude of the force of kinetic friction is given by f = μ N = bxmg and is opposite to the direction of motion. The displacement vector has a magnitude of dx and points to the right, therefore:

\[ W_{d,0} = \int_{0}^{d} \left| \vec{f} \right| \cos180^o \left| \;d\vec{r} \right| = \int_{0}^{d}f\cos180^o\;dx = \int_{0}^{d}-fdx = -\int_{0}^{d}b mg xdx = -b mg \frac{1}{2}x^2 \bigg|_{0}^{d} \]

\[ W_{d,0} = -b mg\frac{d^2}{2} \]

The work is negative because the force is opposite to the direction of the displacement vector.
