Examples: Angular Momentum of Planar Objects

When a planar object, such as those in the figure below, rotates about an axis perpendicular to the plane where it is contained, then the angular momentum about the point of intersection between the axis and the body, point Q in the figure, is parallel to ω and is equal to:

\(\vec{L}_Q = I_{axis}\vec\omega\)

where Iaxis is the moment of inertia measured about the axis of rotation.

In the examples shown in the following figures, the object is contained in the x-y plane and the axis of rotation is perpendicular to the x-y plane and parallel to ω. The axis of rotation intersects the object at point Q, which is at a distance d with respect to the center of mass of the object. Therefore, the moment of inertia about point Q can be obtained using the parallel axis theorem (or for the defining integral for the moment of inertia). If the mass of the object is M and the distance between the axis of rotation and the axis that passes through the center of mass is d, then:

\(I_{Q}= Md^2 + I_{cm}\),

where \(I_{cm}\) is the moment of inertia of the object about its center of mass.
