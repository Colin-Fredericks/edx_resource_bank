Illustrative Example: Two balls attached to a rod - Rotation about an axis passing through its center of mass

In this example we show an important fact:

When a planar object is spinning about an axis passing through its center of mass and perpendicular to the plane of rotation, its angular momentum about any point in the plane of rotation is equal to the angular momentum of the object about its center of mass:

\(\vec{L}_{Q}=I_{cm}\vec{\omega} \)   where Q is any point in the plane of rotation.

This result is valid even if the object is asymmetrical as shown in the following example.

Two balls attached by a rod: Angular Momentum about a Point in the Plane of Rotation.[show]

Let's consider a rigid object consisting of two small balls of masses m1 and m2, m2 > m1, connected by a rod of negligible mass. The object is placed on a horizontal and frictionless table contained in the x-y plane. The object is set to rotate counterclockwise as viewed from above the x-y plane with an angular speed ω hence its axis of rotation is perpendicular to the plane of rotation and passing through the center of mass of the object.

For computational simplicity, we define the distance from the axis to m1 to be d1, and the distance to m2 to be d2.

Case 1: calculate the angular momentum of the rotating object about the axis passing through its the center of mass. The top view of the object is shown in the figure below.

Case 1 - Solution [show]

Consider the origin of the coordinate system to be at the center of the object. The angular momentum of the object with respect to the center of mass is given by the sum of the angular momentum of ball 1 and ball 2. The rod does not have mass therefore it does not contribute to the total angular momentum.

Its magnitude is given by:     \(L_{cm}=d_1m_1v_1+d_2m_2v_2\),   and its direction is out of the screen, along the +z-axis.

Because the particles are both moving with the same angular speed \(\omega\), their tangential speed is given by: \(v_1=\omega d_1\) and \(v_2=\omega d_2\). Replacing these values in the expression of the angular momentum we obtain:

\(L_{cm}=(m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2)\omega=I_{cm}\omega\)

where \(I_{cm}=m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2\)   is the moment of inertia of the object about its center of mass.

Case 2: calculate the angular momentum of the object about the point Q which is at the location of the small ball of mass m1 when the object is along the x axis with ball 1 at the left as shown in the top view of the situation.

Solution - Case 2[show]

Consider the origin of the coordinate system to be at the center of mass of the object. The angular momentum of the object with respect to point Qis given by the sum of the angular momentum of ball 1 and ball 2 about point Q.

Its magnitude is given by:  \(L_{Q}=(d_1+d_2)m_2v_2\), and its direction is out of the screen, along the +z-axis.

The speed of ball 2 is given by:   \(v_2=\omega d_2\). Replacing in the expression of the angular momentum we obtain:

\(L_{Q}=(d_1+d_2)m_2\omega d_2 =(d_1d_2m_2+d_2^2m_2) \omega \)

Adding and subtracting \(d_1^2m1\) inside the parentheses:

\(L_{Q} = (d_1d_2m_2+d_2^2m_2+d_1^2m_1-d_1^2m_1)\omega= \)

\(L_{Q}= (d_1d_2m_2-d_1^2m_1)\omega+(d_1^2m_1+d_2^2m_2)\omega=\)

Inside the second parentheses we recognize the moment of inertia about the center of mass:
\(I_{cm}=m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2\)

\(L_{Q}= d_1(d_2m_2-d_1m_1)\omega+I_{cm}\omega\)    (eq.1)

Because the zero of our coordinate system is at the center of mass, then the position of the center of mass measured from the center of mass is zero. Using the definition, the expression of the center of mass is given by:

\(x_{cm} = (m_1d_1-m_2d_2)/(m_1+m_2)=0\)

therefore,

\(m_1d_1-m_2d_2=0\)    (eq. 2)

Using (eq. 2) in (eq. 1) we obtain that \(L_{Q}=0+I_{cm}\omega\)   which is the angular momentum about the axis passing through the center of mass in Case 1. As a result:

\(L_Q=L_{cm}=I_{cm}\omega\)

Case 3: calculate the angular momentum of the object about the same point Q when the object is along the x axis with ball 1 at the right as shown in the top view of the situation:

Solution - Case 3[show]

Consider the origin of the coordinate system to be at the center of mass of the system. The angular momentum of the object with respect to point Q is given by the sum of the angular momentum of ball 1 and ball 2 about point Q. At this instant of time, the angular momentum of ball 2 with respect to Q is into the screen, along the negative z-axis, whereas the angular momentum of ball 1 with respect to Q is out of the screen, along the positive z-axis. As a result, the angular momentum of the object about point Q is given by:  \(L_{Q}=((d_1+d_2)+(d_1-d_2))m_1v_1-(d_1-d_2)m_2v_2\)

where \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) are the speed of ball 1 and ball 2 and given by:   \(v_1=\omega d_1\) and \(v_2=\omega d_2\). Replacing these in the expression of the angular momentum we obtain:

\(L_{Q}=( ( (d_1+d_2)+(d_1-d_2) )m_1d_1- (d_1-d_2)m_2d_2 ) \omega \)=

\(L_{Q}=( 2m_1d_1^2 - m_2d_1d_2+m_2d_2^2 ) \omega \)=

Adding and subtracting \(m_1d_1^2\) inside the parentheses:

\(L_{Q}=( 2m_1d_1^2 - m_2d_1d_2 + m_2d_2^2+m_1d_1^2-m_1d_1^2 ) \omega \) =

regrouping the terms inside the parentheses we obtain:

\(L_{Q}=( (m_1d_1^2 + m_2d_2^2)+(- m_2d_2 +m_1d_1)d_1 ) \omega \) =

Inside the first parentheses we recognize the moment of inertia about the center of mass, \(I_{cm}=m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2\):

\(L_{Q}= I_{cm}\omega+(- m_2d_2 +m_1d_1)d_1\omega\)    (eq.1)

Because the zero of our coordinate system is at the center of mass, then the position of the center of mass measured from the center of mass is zero. Using the definition, the center of mass is given by:

\(x_{cm} = (m_1d_1-m_2d_2)/(m_1+m_2)=0\)

therefore,

\(m_1d_1-m_2d_2=0\)    (eq. 2)

Using (eq. 2) in (eq. 1) we obtain that \(L_{Q}=I_{cm}\omega+0\)  which is the angular momentum about the axis passing through the center of mass in Case 1. As a result:

\(L_Q=L_{cm}=I_{cm}\omega\)

Solution - Case 4[show]

Consider the origin of the coordinate system to be at the center of mass of the object. The angular momentum of the object with respect to point Q is given by the sum of the angular momentum of ball 1 and ball 2 about point Q. At the instant of time shown in the figure, the angular momentum of both balls with respect to Q is out of the screen, along the positive z axis. As a result, the angular momentum of the object about point Q is given by:

\(L_{Q}= d_1m_1v_1+d_2m_2v_2\)

where \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) are the speed of ball ball 1 and ball 2 and given by:   \(v_1=\omega d_1\) and \(v_2=\omega d_2\). Replacing in the expression of the angular momentum we obtain:

\(L_{Q}=(m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2 ) \omega =\)

\(L_{Q}= I_{cm}\omega\)   where   \(I_{cm}=m_1d_1^2+m_2d_2^2\)   is the moment of inertia of the object about the center of mass. Therefore, the angular momentum about point Q is the angular momentum about the axis passing through the center of mass in Case 1:

\(L_Q=L_{cm}=I_{cm}\omega\)
