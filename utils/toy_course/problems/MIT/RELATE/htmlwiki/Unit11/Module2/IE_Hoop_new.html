Illustrative Example: Angular Momentum of a Hoop

Consider a hoop of mass M and radius R contained in the xy-plane as shown below. The hoop is rotating with angular velocity ω, about an axis perpendicular to the plane of the hoop and passing through point Q, the point of intersection between the axis of rotation and the plane. Calculate the angular momentum of the hoop about point Q

Answer: [show]

\(\vec{L}_Q=2MR^2\vec{\omega}\)

Solution: [show]

In this particular problem:

  1. the object is planar
  2. the object rotates in the plane on which it is contained
  3. the axis of rotation is perpendicular to the plane of rotation
  4. the axis passes through a point in the object (point Q)

Therefore, the angular momentum about the axis passing through point Q is given by:

\(\vec{L}_Q=I_Q\vec{\omega}\)

where \(I_Q\) is the moment of inertia about point Q which is calculated by using the parallel axis theorem. Because the axis passing through point Q is at a distance \(R\) with respect to the center of mass of the hoop, then:

\(I_{Q}=MR^2+I_{cm}^{hoop}=MR^2+MR^2 =2MR^2\)

Therefore, \(\vec{L}_Q= 2MR^2\vec{\omega}\)
