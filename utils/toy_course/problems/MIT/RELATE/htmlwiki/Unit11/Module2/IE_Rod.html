Illustrative Example: Angular Momentum of a Rod

Consider a rod of length D with mass M distributed uniformly throughout it. The moment of inertia of the rod about one of its ends is \(MD^2/3\). The rod rotates within the x-y plane with an angular speed ω. Calculate the angular momentum about:

  1. the axis perpendicular to the x-y plane passing through the center of mass (left figure)
  2. the axis perpendicular to the x-y plane passing through point Q (right figure).

Answer: [show]
1. About the axis passing through the center of mass: Lcm = (MD2/12)ω.

2. About the axis passing through point Q: LQ = (MD2/3)ω.

Solution [show]

The angular momentum about the center of mass is parallel to \(\omega\) and its magnitude is given by:

\(L_{cm}=I_{cm}\omega\)

We are given the moment of inertia about one end, \(I_Q=MD^2/3\), and it is related to the moment of inertia about the center of mass through the parallel axis theorem.:

\(I_Q=M(D/2)^2+I_{cm}=M(D/2)^2+I_{cm}\)

Therefore, the moment of inertia about the center of mass of the rod:

\(I_{cm}=I_Q-M(D/2)^2=MD^2/3 - MD^2/4 = MD^2/12\)

The angular momentum about the center of mass is parallel to \(\omega\) and its magnitude is:

\(L_{cm}=MD^2/12\omega\)

and the angular momentum about the axis passing through point Q is parallel to \(\omega\) and its magnitude is:

\(L_Q=MD^2/3\omega\)
