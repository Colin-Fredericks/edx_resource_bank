Illustrative Example: A Pivoted Rod

A rod of mass m and length D is pivoted about one end. As shown in the figure, the rod rotates in the x-y plane counterclockwise as viewed from the +z axis with speed ω.

Let Icm be the moment of inertia about an axis passing through the rod's center of mass, perpendicular to the x-y plane, and Ipivot the moment of inertia about an axis passing through the pivot point perpendicular to the x-y plane.

The Kinetic Energy of the rod is:

1. \(K = \frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2\)

2. \(K = \frac{1}{2} mV_{cm}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2\)

3. \(K = \frac{1}{2} mV_{cm}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_{pivot}\omega^2\)

4. \(K = \frac{1}{2}I_{pivot}\omega^2\)

Answer [show]
5. Both 2 and 4 are correct
Explanation [show]
Fixed axis rotation (option 4):

The rod rotates about a fixed axis passing through the pivot point. The motion of the rod is contained in the x-y plane, perpendicular to the axis of rotation. The kinetic energy of a rigid object rotating about a fixed axis is:

\(K = \frac{1}{2}I_{pivot}\omega^2 \)     (eq. 1)

where Ipivot is the moment of inertia of the rod about the axis passing through the pivot point ( Ipivot=mD2/3).

Another point of view (option 2):

We can also think that the rod is rotating and translating and calculate kinetic energy of the rod as the sum of the translational and the rotational kinetic energy:

\(K =\frac{1}{2} mV_{cm}^2+\frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2 \)     (eq. 2)

where \(V_{cm}\) is the speed of the center of mass.


Prove that options 2 and 4 are the same

We can show that (eq. 1) and (eq. 2) are the same by using the following facts:


1. The axes passing through the pivot point and through the center of mass are parallel and are separated a distance D/2. Then, the parallel axis theorem implies:

\(I_{pivot}=I_{cm}+m(D/2)^2\)     (eq. 3)


2. The center of mass is a point of the rigid object therefore, as any other point of the rod, it rotates about the pivot with angular speed ω. The speed of the center of mass can be expressed as:

\(v_{cm}=\omega\frac{D}{2}\)            (eq. 4)

Using (eq.4) in (eq. 2):

\(K =\frac{1}{2} m(\omega \frac{D}{2})^2+\frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2=\frac{1}{2}(m\frac{D^2}{4}+I_{cm})\omega^2 \)

According to (eq. 3), the expression inside the parenthesis is the moment of inertia of the rod about the pivot point, therefore:

\(K =\frac{1}{2} mV_{cm}^2+\frac{1}{2}I_{cm}\omega^2 =\frac{1}{2}I_{pivot}\omega^2\)
