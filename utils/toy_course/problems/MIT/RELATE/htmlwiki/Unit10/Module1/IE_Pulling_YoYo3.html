Illustrative Example: Pulling a Yo-Yo

working on this one

Consider a yo-yo consisting of two solid disks, each of the same mass and radius a, connected by a central spindle of radius b and negligible mass. The total mass of the yo-yo is M. A light string is coiled around the central spindle; we shall assume that its thickness is sufficiently small that winding or unwinding has no effect on the effective radius of the spindle. The moment of inertia of the yo-yo about an axis passing through its center of mass and perpendicular to the screen is \(I_{cm}\).

In figures 1 and 2 the yo-yo is placed upright on a horizontal and frictionless surface, gravity is parallel to the screen pointing down. In figure 3, the yo-yo is placed flat on the surface, gravity is into the screen. The string is pulled with a constant force of magnitude T with the direction shown in the figures.

For each of the three situations, What is the magnitude and the direction of the center of mass acceleration and the angular acceleration? Consider the +x axis to be in the direction of the tension.

Answer: [show]

Center of mass acceleration:
Is the same for the three cases. Its magnitude is \(T/M\), and its direction is along the +x axis.

Angular acceleration:
The magnitude is the same for the three cases and equal to \(bT/I_{cm}\).
The direction is out of the screen, counter-clockwise rotation, for cases 1 and 3, and is into the screen, clockwise rotation, for case 2.
